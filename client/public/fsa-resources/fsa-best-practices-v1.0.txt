SCREENING QUESTIONS AT INTAKE:
1. "What types of income did you receive?" (W-2, 1099, self-employment?)
2. "Will you take the standard deduction or itemize?" (Most don't itemize)
3. "How comfortable are you using a computer?" (Scale 1-5)
4. "Do you have internet access at home?" (If no, offer on-site FSA)
5. "Have you filed taxes before? Any IRS letters?" (Prior issues?)

COMMUNICATION CHANNELS
Phone Support: Primary method for quick questions
- Toll-free number: 1-800-MD-TAXES (1-800-638-2937)
- Answer within 3 rings during posted hours
- Professional greeting: "Maryland VITA FSA Support, this is [Name]. How can I help you today?"

Video/Screen Sharing: For complex issues requiring visual guidance
- Platforms: Zoom, Microsoft Teams, or Google Meet
- Schedule 30-minute slots to avoid rushing
- Send calendar invite with meeting link in advance
- Test screen sharing before taxpayer session

Email Support: For non-urgent questions or document review
- Response time: Within 4 hours during business hours
- Use secure email for any tax documents (encrypted)
- Template responses for common questions (see Appendix)

REMOTE SUPPORT BEST PRACTICES
âœ“ Build Rapport First
   - "Thank you for using FSA! I'm here to help."
   - Ask open-ended questions: "What step are you working on?"
   - Patience is key - many taxpayers are nervous about taxes

âœ“ Active Listening
   - Paraphrase their concern: "So you're unsure about entering your W-2 Box 1?"
   - Avoid jargon - use plain language: "gross income" = "total pay before taxes"
   - Let them finish speaking before jumping in

âœ“ Guide, Don't Do
   - "Let me walk you through it" (not "let me do it for you")
   - Ask: "Do you see the button that says 'Add W-2'?" (interactive)
   - Empower them: "You're doing great! That's exactly right."

âœ“ Screen Sharing Etiquette
   - Ask permission: "Would you mind sharing your screen so I can see what you see?"
   - Respect privacy: "I'll only look at the tax form, not other windows"
   - Annotate carefully: Use screen pointer/arrows, not mouse control
   - End sharing when done: "You can stop sharing now, thank you!"

âœ“ Document Interactions
   - Log each support session: Date, time, issue, resolution
   - Note if follow-up needed or complex case flagged
   - Track common issues for training improvements

â˜ FINAL CHECKS
   â˜ Refund/balance due amount reasonable
   â˜ Direct deposit info verified (if refund)
   â˜ Payment plan setup (if balance due)
   â˜ No math errors flagged by software

REVIEW DELIVERY METHODS
1. Virtual Review (most common):
   - Taxpayer shares screen via Zoom/Teams
   - Navigator walks through return page by page
   - Takes 20-30 minutes on average

2. Appointment Review:
   - Taxpayer brings printed return to site
   - Navigator reviews in person
   - Good for complex cases or elderly taxpayers

3. Email Review (use cautiously):
   - Taxpayer emails PDF of return (password-protected)
   - Navigator reviews and calls with feedback
   - Only for simple returns, no sensitive data via email

âœ“ Maryland credits: Earned Income, Child Tax, Poverty Level
