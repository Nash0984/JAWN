WHAT IS FSA?
Free Self-Assisted (FSA) tax preparation allows eligible taxpayers to prepare
their own federal and state returns using free TaxSlayer Pro software, with
navigator support available remotely or on-site.

1. FUSION SITE (Hybrid Model)
   Description: Offers both in-person VITA and FSA at same location
   
   Workflow:
   - Intake desk screens taxpayers: FSA or in-person?
   - FSA taxpayers directed to self-service workstations
   - In-person taxpayers meet with preparers
   - Navigators assist both FSA users and in-person quality review
   
   Staffing: 2+ navigators (1 for FSA, 1+ for in-person prep)
   
   Ideal For: High-volume sites, variety of taxpayer skill levels

2. STAND-ALONE FSA SITE
   Description: FSA-only location, no in-person prep available
   
   Workflow:
   - Taxpayers walk in, check in at desk
   - Navigator provides quick orientation (5 min)
   - Taxpayer works independently on FSA workstation
   - Navigator available on-site for questions
   - Optional: Navigator reviews return before filing
   
   Staffing: 1-2 navigators (roving support)
   
   Ideal For: Areas with limited volunteers, tech-savvy populations

3. REMOTE FSA SUPPORT HUB
   Description: Navigators support off-site taxpayers via phone/video
   
   Workflow:
   - Taxpayers access FSA from home
   - Call toll-free number or email for help
   - Navigator provides remote guidance (screen share)
   - Scheduled appointments for quality review
   
   Staffing: 2-3 navigators (call center style)
   
   Ideal For: Rural areas, COVID-safe operations, extended hours

NAVIGATOR ROLE IN EACH MODEL
All models: Coach, answer questions, quality review
Fusion: Also coordinate between FSA and in-person
Stand-Alone: Roving assistance, technical troubleshooting
Remote: Phone/video proficiency, screen sharing expertise

COMMON SCREEN-BY-SCREEN GUIDANCE

STEP 1: PERSONAL INFORMATION
- Name: Must match Social Security card exactly (including Jr., Sr.)
- SSN: No dashes, just 9 digits (TaxSlayer auto-formats)
- Address: Current mailing address (where IRS will send correspondence)
- Phone: Best contact number (for IRS if needed)
- Email: Valid email for account recovery

Navigator Tip: Stress importance of accuracy - errors delay refunds!

STEP 2: FILING STATUS
- Single: Unmarried on Dec 31
- Married Filing Jointly: Most common for married couples
- Married Filing Separately: Rare, usually worse tax outcome
- Head of Household: Unmarried with qualifying dependent + paid >50% home costs
- Qualifying Surviving Spouse: Widow(er) within 2 years, with dependent

Navigator Tip: Head of Household is often misunderstood. Verify:
  1. Unmarried on Dec 31 (or living apart from spouse last 6 months)
  2. Paid over half the home costs (rent, utilities, food)
  3. Qualifying child lived with them >6 months

STEP 3: DEPENDENTS
- Full Name and SSN required
- Relationship: Child, stepchild, sibling, parent, etc.
- Date of Birth: Determines age-based credits (Child Tax Credit if <17)
- Months Lived With You: Must be >6 months (or all year for foster)
- Did Child Provide Own Support?: Usually "No"

Navigator Tip: "Dependent already claimed" error = someone else claimed them
  Action: Verify who should claim (custodial parent in divorce)

STEP 4: INCOME
W-2 Wage Income (most common):
- Click "Add W-2"
- Enter exactly as shown on form:
  â€¢ Box 1: Wages, tips, other compensation
  â€¢ Box 2: Federal income tax withheld
  â€¢ Boxes 3-6: Social Security and Medicare wages/withholding
  â€¢ Boxes 15-17: State income, tax withheld, locality
- Repeat for each W-2 (multiple jobs)

1099 Income (if applicable):
- 1099-INT: Interest income (bank, savings)
- 1099-DIV: Dividend income (stocks)
- 1099-MISC: Miscellaneous income (Box 3 = other income)
- 1099-NEC: Nonemployee compensation (self-employment - FLAG FOR REVIEW!)
- 1099-G: Unemployment compensation
- 1099-R: Retirement distributions (pensions, IRA)

Navigator Tip: Self-employment (1099-NEC) = complex, refer to in-person

STEP 5: DEDUCTIONS
Standard Deduction (90%+ of FSA users):
- $13,850 (Single)
- $27,700 (Married Filing Jointly)
- $20,800 (Head of Household)
- Automatically selected by software

Itemized Deductions (rarely better for low-income):
- Mortgage interest (Form 1098)
- Charitable contributions (receipts required)
- State/local taxes (SALT, $10k cap)
- Medical expenses (>7.5% AGI)

Navigator Tip: Software compares standard vs itemized, picks better option

STEP 6: CREDITS
Earned Income Tax Credit (EIC):
- Largest credit for low-income workers
- Requires qualifying child OR age 25-64 (if no child)
- Software calculates automatically based on income + dependents
- Audit risk if claimed incorrectly - verify eligibility!

Child Tax Credit (CTC):
- $2,000 per child under 17
- Partially refundable ($1,600 Additional Child Tax Credit)
- Income limits apply (phaseout at $200k/$400k)

Other Dependent Credit:
- $500 per qualifying dependent (not child, e.g., elderly parent)

Education Credits:
- American Opportunity Credit: Up to $2,500 (first 4 years college)
- Lifetime Learning Credit: Up to $2,000 (any education)
- Requires Form 1098-T (tuition statement)

Child & Dependent Care Credit:
- Up to $3,000 for one child, $6,000 for two+
- Daycare, after-school, summer camp costs
- Must provide childcare provider's name, address, EIN

Navigator Tip: Education credits often missed - ask "Did you pay tuition?"

STEP 7: REFUND OR PAYMENT
If Refund:
- Direct Deposit (fastest, 10-21 days): Enter bank routing + account number
- Paper Check (slower, 4-6 weeks): Sent to mailing address

If Balance Due:
- Pay Now (bank withdrawal): Authorize IRS to debit account
- Mail Check: Form 1040-V payment voucher
- Payment Plan: IRS installment agreement (if large amount)

Navigator Tip: Verify bank account info CAREFULLY (wrong account = lost refund!)

STEP 8: E-FILE
- Review summary page (errors highlighted)
- Enter 5-digit Self-Select PIN (any 5 numbers except all zeros)
- Spouse also enters PIN (if Married Filing Jointly)
- Click "Submit Return"
- Confirmation screen: "Accepted by IRS" (save/print confirmation!)

Navigator Tip: Rejection common reasons = SSN mismatch, dependent already claimed

ESCALATION TIERS

TIER 1: LEAD NAVIGATOR (On-Site or Available by Phone)
When to escalate:

- Taxpayer dispute/complaint
- Uncertain if case is FSA-appropriate

When to escalate:
- System-wide technical failure (internet down, TaxSlayer outage)
- Taxpayer safety concern (threatening, distressed)
- Volunteer/staff issue (navigator absent, inadequate staffing)
- Policy question (can we serve non-Maryland residents?)

Example: "All workstations lost internet - 5 taxpayers waiting. What do we do?"

Coordinator Response: Emergency troubleshooting, reschedule taxpayers, IT support

TIER 3: IRS/TAXSLAYER/MARYLAND VITA SUPPORT
When to escalate:
- Critical software bug affecting many taxpayers
- Suspected fraud or identity theft
- Legal/compliance question (SIDN usage, data breach)
- Unresolved technical issue

Example: "TaxSlayer is rejecting all Maryland state e-files with error code 9876."

Support Response: Technical fix, workaround guidance, escalate to vendor
